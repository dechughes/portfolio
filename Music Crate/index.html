    About this crate

    This interactive crate is a personal project based on the Soulquarians - an informal name given to a loose collective of musicians that formed around Questlove, D’Angelo, James Poyser and J Dilla. Often described as a melting pot for musical experimentation, the artists working under this banner helped define the neo-soul movement and shaped the sound of soul, jazz and hip-hop in the late ’90s and early 2000s.

    I wanted to recreate some of the playful, exploratory feel of early internet design - sites like the original Space Jam website for the 1996 film (which also featured a great D’Angelo track on the soundtrack). They weren’t especially polished or commercial, but they felt like places where the creators wanted to offer something extra, rather than simply push content.

    I’ve always liked the idea of flicking through records, even though it was slightly before my time. There’s something appealing about picking up a record or a book you know nothing about, where the cover, the setting and a bit of curiosity do most of the work - rather than lists, rankings or recommendations.

    This is version one of the project. It’s mainly an experiment in UI and interaction, with a bit of storytelling mixed in. The aim was to make something that feels closer to a proper record shop visit, rather than just clicking through content. Credit to AI for help with parts of the code, and to the sources below for visual and musical reference.
