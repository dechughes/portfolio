This section evaluates the user funnel from acquisition through activation and conversion to identify where value is lost and which stages most constrain revenue growth. The goal is to understand channel quality, behavioural drop‑offs, and commercial bottlenecks.

---

## **Key Questions**

- Is acquisition volume high but quality low?  
- Do certain channels activate or convert better?  
- Where is the biggest drop‑off in the funnel?  
- What percentage of sign‑ups ever generate revenue?  
- Is the business reliant on a single channel?  
- Which stage would drive the most revenue if improved?

## **Findings from Funnel Analysis**

### **Acquisition Quality**

Organic is the largest driver of users (40k), followed by Paid Social (30k), Referral (20k), and Influencers (10k). Activation and conversion rates vary by less than 0.01% across channels, indicating consistent user quality regardless of acquisition source.

---

### **Activation → Conversion Drop‑off**

Activation is strong at ~60%, but only ~41.5% of activated users convert to paid. This is the largest behavioural drop‑off in the funnel. Because activation and conversion are both stable across channels, activation does not meaningfully predict conversion.

---

### **Efficiency**

24.83% of all sign‑ups ever generate revenue within the 2024 dataset. It is worth noting this figure slightly understates the true lifetime proportion - late‑year sign‑ups who converted in November or December will continue generating revenue into 2025 but fall outside the dataset boundary.

---

### **Commercial Bottleneck**

The primary constraint to revenue growth is the conversion stage. Users take ~56 days to convert and users only generate revenue when they are at this stage; therefore improving conversion speed and rates would have the largest commercial impact.

---

## **Summary**

The funnel shows strong and stable acquisition across all channels, with no meaningful differences in activation or conversion quality. The largest behavioural drop‑off occurs between activation and conversion, where only 41.5% of activated users become paid users. Because conversion speed is slow (~56 days), the primary commercial bottleneck is the conversion stage. Improving conversion speed and rate would have the greatest impact on revenue growth.
